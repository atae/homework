Ingredients

  • 1 (15 oz.) can chickpeas
  • 2 tbsp Olive Oil
  • 1 to 2 tbsp Sriracha
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/8 tsp cumin

With regards to Tahini, you can easily make your own by roasting and blending some sesame seeds and it will also taste better than any Tahini you can buy. However, hot sesame seeds can melt any container you put it in, so either be careful when you roast sesame seeds or pony up for some tahini. It will be worth it either way.

Sriracha Hummus

Description

This is a simple recipe that combines two great concepts: hummus and sriracha. I've taken this recipe off the internet (budgetbytes in particular), but the recipe is so simple that there may really be no other good way to make hummus.

I came across this recipe after being disgruntled with finding a hummus in stores that was better than the one I had at a local farmer's market. It turns out that making your own hummus is the only way to get really good hummus. Who would've thought?

Directions

  1. Drain the chickpeas
  2. Combine everything in the food processor/blender.
  3. Process until smooth. You can add more olive oil or water if it's not creamy enough.
  4. Eat!
